🎯 Learning Outcomes

UWP1 learning outcomes are grounded in the Council of Writing Program Administrators Outcomes Statement for First-Year Composition. They focus on five areas:

🗣️

Rhetorical Concepts

Understand audience, purpose, context, mode, genre, discourse community, revision, and editing — and how your grasp of these evolves.

✍️

Reading & Writing Processes

Practice reading, researching, and composing as social, recursive processes. Revise based on peer and instructor feedback across multiple drafts.

📏

Knowledge of Conventions

Explore form, language, and format expectations across discourse communities — connecting your home communities to academic, civic, and professional ones.

🔍

Research

Collect and analyze data, engage prior knowledge, and make new meaning by working with and across sources and communities.

🪞

Metacognition

Reflect on your rhetorical choices, composing processes, and your growth as a writer to build lasting confidence and skills.

Note: Activities include class discussions, reading response journals, an extended research project, and reflective process memos. University policy requires 6,000 words of original work.

📜 Policies

Attendance & Participation

This is a workshop and discussion course — not a lecture course. Regular, active attendance is essential because much of the learning happens during classroom discussions and peer response workshops. Students who miss the first week will be dropped. Please bring your laptop and/or tablet to every class.

Academic Integrity & Citations

The prerequisite for UWP 1 is completion of the Entry Level Writing Requirement (ELWR). You must earn a C- or higher to fulfill the lower-division writing requirement. We will discuss ethical citation throughout the course. Intentional plagiarism will be reported to SJA. See the UC Davis Student Code of Conduct.

Writing Requirement

More information about writing requirements: elw.ucdavis.edu.

Incomplete Policy

Incompletes are only granted for documented emergencies (serious illness, death in the family). Please reach out immediately if such a situation arises. If planning to take P/NP, consult your major advisor first.

💚 Diversity & Inclusion: This course is committed to a safe, intellectually challenging environment for all students regardless of race, ethnicity, documentation status, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, language, or religion. Written accents, like spoken accents, do not represent intelligence and will not result in lower grades. Use of other languages is encouraged.

If any aspect of this course creates barriers to your inclusion, please let Prof. O know as soon as possible. Students with learning differences are encouraged to connect with the Student Disability Center.

⚖️ Grading

This course uses contract grading — a model popular in writing studies that reduces the subjectivity of evaluating writing and focuses you on learning and hard work rather than trying to guess what the teacher wants.

In traditional grading you're judged on your final drafts. In contract grading, you're rewarded for your labor. A midterm contract grade update will be provided.

"Your labor, your attitude, and your growth are as important as the quality of your final products. I want your grade to reflect the holistic you."

Grades are based on the grading contract criteria — not a curve or a set distribution. Full details will be shared in class and on Canvas.

📝 Assignments

📖

Course Readings

All readings are on Canvas in various formats (PDFs, links, videos). Contact Prof. O if you need an alternate format.

💬

Class Discussions

Come to class having annotated the readings — underline key ideas, write questions in the margins. You can annotate digitally using PDF highlight/comment tools, Notability, or Goodnotes. Videos don't require annotation.

📅

Weekend Reflection Homework

Every Saturday, submit a 150–300 word reflection based on a weekly prompt. These are low-stakes, exploratory posts — don't stress over grammar. Prof. O may share them to spark class discussions, so only write what you're comfortable sharing.

✍️

One Major Writing Assignment Required to pass

There is one major writing assignment. Some weekend reflections will feed into this final project. Full details will be discussed in class and posted to Canvas.

💻

Digital Project Required for an A

A digital transformation of your major writing assignment. Students who complete the digital project are eligible for an A in the course. Details to be shared in class.
